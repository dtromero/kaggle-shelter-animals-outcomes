The Spanish Mastiff is a giant breed of dog, originating in Spain. Originally bred to be a guard dog, its purpose was to defend livestock from wolves and other predators.

The Spanish Mastiff is a large and powerful dog, similar in appearance to the other mastiff breeds. They have a large powerful head and serious and vigilant expression

Males in this breed are 70 to 85 centimetres (28 to 33 in) tall at the withers, and range from 50 to 70 kilograms (110 to 150 lb). Females are at least 65 centimetres (26 in), and weigh 40 to 60 kilograms (88 to 132 lb).[1]

It has small eyes and distinctive ears resembling triangles. The coat is usually fawn; may be brindle, black or grey.
